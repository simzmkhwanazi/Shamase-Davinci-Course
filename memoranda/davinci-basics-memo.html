Q001 · Module 01

A5
B6
C7
D8
✓ WHY C IS CORRECT
Correct: Media, Cut, Edit, Fusion, Color, Fairlight, Deliver.
Example. Open Resolve and look along the bottom bar — you'll count seven icons: Media, Cut, Edit, Fusion, Color, Fairlight, Deliver, each a full workspace.
Why the other options are incorrect

BClose but missing one — there are 7, not 6.
DToo many — there are 7 main pages.
Principle: The 7 pages: Media, Cut, Edit, Fusion, Color, Fairlight, Deliver.
Q002 · Module 01
Which of these is NOT one of Resolve's main pages?
ACut
BCompose
CFusion
DFairlight
✓ WHY B IS CORRECT
Correct: there is no 'Compose' page in Resolve.
Example. If a tutorial says 'go to the Compose page,' it's wrong — that compositing work actually happens on the Fusion page.
Why the other options are incorrect
ACut is a real page — used for fast editing with dual timelines.
CFusion is a real page — used for compositing and VFX.
DFairlight is a real page — used for audio mixing.
Principle: The 7 pages don't include a Compose page — Fusion handles that work.
Q003 · Module 01

BFairlight
CDeliver
DEdit
✓ WHY C IS CORRECT
Correct: Deliver is where you render the final file — the last step.
Example. Finishing a wedding film: cut on Edit, grade on Color, mix on Fairlight, then switch to Deliver last to render the MP4 you hand the client.
Why the other options are incorrect
AColor comes before audio mixing in the standard workflow.
BFairlight (audio) comes before Deliver but isn't the last step.
DEdit comes early — picture lock happens here before grading.
Principle: Workflow order: Media → Cut/Edit → Fusion → Color → Fairlight → Deliver.
